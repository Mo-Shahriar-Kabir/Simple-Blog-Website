Blog Posts

Blog 1

Why Learning HTML First Matters

Before frameworks and fancy tooling, the browser only understands HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. A solid grasp of semantic HTML makes your pages more accessible, easier to style, and kinder to search engines. Master the raw materials and every framework you meet will feel less mysterious.

Blog 2

CSS: From Chaos to Consistency

Most CSS pain comes from mixing structure and decoration. Group related styles, name things clearly, and lean on layout primitives like Flexbox and Grid. Consistency beats cleverness: predictable patterns free you to focus on design instead of firefighting overrides.

Blog 3

JavaScript: Writing Intent, Not Tricks

Modern JavaScript offers endless shortcuts, but clarity wins long term. Prefer readable functions over nested callbacks, use descriptive variable names, and embrace small refactors early. Code that communicates intent is easier to optimize, test, and extend.
